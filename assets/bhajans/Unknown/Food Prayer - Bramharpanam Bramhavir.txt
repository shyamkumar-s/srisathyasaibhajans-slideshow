Food Prayer - Bramharpanam Bramhavir
Prayer
			Meaning
			
	
	
		
			
				Oh Lord, Thou art the food,
				Thou art the enjoyer of the food,
				Thou art the giver and preparer of the food.
				Along with the inward and outward breaths
				You are the digestive fire that digests all types of food.
				We offer to You, all that we consume.
			
			
				
				
			
		
                
			
				Brahmarpanam Brahmahavir
				Brahmagnou Brahmanahutam
				Brahmaivatena Gantavyam
				Brahmakarma Samadhina
			
			
				
					The act of offering is God. The offering is God.
					By God it is offered into the Fire of God.
					God is That which is to be attained by him
					who performs action pertaining to God.
					– Bhagavad Geeta, Chapter 4, Verse 24
				
				
					
						
					
				
			
		
		
			
				Aham Vaishvanaro Bhutva
				Praninam Dehamashritaha
				Pranapana Samayuktaha
				Pachamyannam Chaturvidham
			
			
				I reside as Vaishvanaro, the digestive power, in every living being.
				I keep in balance the Prana (life breath) and Apana (downward moving air).
				I digest and assimilate the four types of food partaken by every living being.
				– Bhagavad Geeta, Chapter 15, Verse 14
				For a description of the five vital airs in the body, please read Sutra Vahini, Chapter 8, or, Summer Showers 1991 - Upanishads, Discourse 7.
